About

I am a Ph.D. student in Bioengineering at the University of Washington. My research combines electrophysiology, neural data analysis, and computational modeling to study cortical plasticity, neuromodulation, and brain-computer interfaces.

I am especially interested in translational neurotechnology: building tools and models that can help make stimulation and neural interface systems more adaptive, targeted, and effective.

Research Interests

Neurotechnology, brain stimulation, neural plasticity, BCI, signal processing

Methods

EEG, ECoG, electrophysiology, MATLAB, Python, modeling, statistics

Current Focus

Inferring cortical plasticity rules and improving stimulation design
